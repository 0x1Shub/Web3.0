6) Accounts on Solana
- On the solana blockchain, an "Account" is a fundamental data structure used to store various types of information.
- Data Storage: Accounts on solana are used to store data required by programs (smart contracts) or to maintain state
- Lamports: Accounts hold a balance of Solana native cyptocurrency, lamports. Lamports are used pay for transaction fees and to rent the space that account occupies on the blockchain.
- Programs: On Solana, programs are special accounts that contain executable code. These accounts are distict from regular data accounts in that they are designed to be executable by blockchain when trigger by transaction.

7) Types of Accounts
- Wallet Account : Holds SOL (lamports)
- Data Account : Stores app data
- Program Account: Stores executable code
- Token Account (special case): Stores token balances (via Token Program)

When you are creating accounts in blockchain. You need to have some lamports in accounts otherwise it will close.

9) How to give value to our own created token ?? like 1 ABC = 1000 rupess
- You do not manually “assign” lasting value to your token.
- The market discovers value through trading, liquidity, demand, trust, and utility.


9) What is Liquidity Pool ??
- A liquidity pool is a basket of two assets locked in a decentralized exchange.
Example: ABC token & Solana (SOL)
You deposit both into pool: 1000 ABC & 10 SOL
Now users can swap: SOL → ABC & ABC → SOL
without needing another buyer directly.

10) How ABC Token Gets a Price ??
If pool has: 1000 ABC & 10 SOL
Then starting implied price: 1 ABC = 0.01 SOL
If 1 SOL = $2500 (your example), then: 1 ABC = $25
But this changes when people trade.
